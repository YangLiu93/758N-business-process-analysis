Beijing Noodles



Beijing Noodles are traditional cooked wheaten food. It originates from Beijing, and is now popular among Northern China, and even South Korea and Japan. Beijing Noodles are low in calories, high in protein and carbohydrate.
Beijing Noodles are combination of soybean paste, noodles, and a lot of seasonal vegetables such as cut cucumber, Chinese toona fruit, bean sprouts, yellow beans. Soybean paste is the most important ingredient. It is made of cooked pork with ginger, green onion, and sweet bean sauce.

Shanxi Sliced Noodles



Shanxi Sliced Noodles are one kind of cooked wheaten food in North China. The special cooking process, in which a curved knife is used to shave the dough into sliced noodles before being put into the boiled water, is its claim to fame in China.
The sauce of Shanxi Sliced Noodles (commonly known as "toppings") is also varied. It could made with tomato sauce, meat fried sauce, mutton soup, lily fungus or eggs, which are very popular with noodle lovers.



Relation: Beijing Noodles and Shanxi Sliced Noodles are both noodles that are extremely popular in North China. They both consist of pork and different kinds of vegetables, provide a variety of nutrients and are good for health.
